# parseVK Product Context

## Register

Product. parseVK is an internal analytical workspace: a beautiful, cinematic dark studio for analysts who inspect social data daily. Design serves craft, speed and visual confidence.

## Product Purpose

parseVK helps analysts collect, inspect, compare and interpret intelligence from VK, OK, Telegram and related sources. The frontend is a workplace, not a SaaS dashboard. It should feel like a mission control room for data: dark, deliberate, dramatic where it matters.

Analysts spend hours here every day. The interface must reward that time with beauty, not just utility. Data should feel alive — glowing metrics, clear hierarchies, visual rhythm that makes large datasets feel controllable and even cinematic.

## Primary Users

- Analysts who check groups, authors, comments and keywords daily. This is their primary workspace.
- Team members who compare authors, posts, threads and export findings for offline reporting.
- Administrators who manage users, credentials, parsing tasks and system health.
- Developers who use logs, task states and diagnostics to understand failures.

## Core Workflows

- Scan the dashboard for key metrics: total comments today, new matches, watchlist changes, parsing health.
- Monitor groups, comments, authors and keyword matches with enough density to scan many rows without losing context.
- Drill from summary views into author, group, task or comment details with smooth transitions and preserved context.
- Export data in predictable formats with clear progress feedback.
- Manage automation settings, Telegram sessions and admin users.
- Review empty, loading and error states — these are moments of truth, not afterthoughts.

## Product Personality

parseVK should feel **cinematic, calm and confident**. Not playful, not cold. The interface earns its visual ambition through precision: every glow has a reason, every metric tells a story, every transition feels considered.

The voice is concise and factual:

- "Task failed", not "Something went wrong".
- "No comments match these filters", not "Nothing to see here".
- "Export CSV", not "Get your data".
- "Retry sync", not "Try again later" when an immediate retry exists.

## Visual Principles

- **Dark is the canvas, not the theme.** Deep blacks let data glow. Use light as a material: soft glows on metrics, subtle gradients on surfaces, light that follows data importance.
- **Data is the hero.** Every visual decision serves data readability. Charts, metrics, comparison views are first-class citizens. Tables and lists support them.
- **Rhythm over density.** Generous spacing between data panels. Let the layout breathe. Density is for tables, not for the page.
- **Kinetic, not static.** Purposeful motion guides the eye: metrics that pulse on update, panels that ease in, hover states that reveal detail.
- **Consistency is a feature, not a constraint.** The visual vocabulary is rich but repeatable. Once a metric card looks a certain way, it looks that way everywhere.

## Strategic Principles

- Make the dashboard the anchor. Every analysts starts and returns here.
- Preserve context while drilling down. Smooth transitions, shared element animations, breadcrumbs that feel like navigation.
- Make system state explicit and beautiful. Running, queued, failed, stale and completed states deserve visual treatment, not just badge colors.
- Treat exports as analytical handoffs. Confirm scope, format and result with ceremony.
- Keep admin and settings screens quiet. These are utility spaces, not stage.

## Anti-Goals

- Do not sacrifice readability for decoration. Cinematic does not mean unusable.
- Do not over-animate. Motion must have purpose: state change, attention direction, data update.
- Do not hide data behind decorative layers. Data is always the point.
- Do not use playful or unserious empty states.
- Do not use color without meaning. Every saturated pixel earns its place.
